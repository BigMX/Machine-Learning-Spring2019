Introduction

Sarcasm is an ironic remark tempered by humor. Mainly, people use sarcasm to say the opposite of what's true to make someone look or feel foolish. For example, when something bad happens, a person might say "Oh, this is exactly what I need to cheer me up!". This kind of tone is easily identified as a sarcasm by human brains, even though the entire sentence is in absence of an explicit negation marker. Now, human is slow in reading long paper, one might cast a question that can we build a machine that can detect sarcasm for us and make the process quicker?

That's exactly what we are going to do in this lab. We will use a dataset that consists of comments from reddit. The dataset has over one million comments with their parent comment(the context). The dataset is a csv file which consists of two sequence data in the columns of "comment" and "parent_comment".The dataset is balanced and has two labels, 0 for normal comment and 1 sarcastic comment. We will use this dataset to train many-to-one RNN models

Business Use

Our model for detect sarcastic comment might find its place in an online plagiarism detection application. Currently, the online plagiarism detection scans documents and detect even similarities and generate the list of original sources. With this algorithm, the plagiarism detection can only determine the similarities of words, rather than the sentimental tones. Right now, the detection flags many people's work as plagiarism even though they are simply making sarcastic sentences, which conveys an opposite meaning against the source. Our model can help detect the sarcastic sentences and it can help with improving the plagiarism dectection.

Metrics

The model return positive if it identifies the sentence is sarcastism and vice versa.

n this lab, we will use Precision as our evaluation metric because we most care about False Positive rate. In our business case, our model is used to detect sarcastism in a plagirism detection. The pain of loss is huge if we identify the work as sarcastic when the work is actually a plagirism. (False Positive). However it is okay for us to miss a few sarcastic comments and flag them as plagirithm. (False Negative). However, we will track the false negative rate and make sure it is not too low.

In [ ]:
import keras.backend as K
def precision_m(y_true, y_pred):
        true_positives = K.sum(K.round(K.clip(y_true * y_pred, 0, 1)))
        predicted_positives = K.sum(K.round(K.clip(y_pred, 0, 1)))
        precision = true_positives / (predicted_positives + K.epsilon())
        return precision

Modeling

In this part, we will develop seven RNN models and find their performance to classify the sarcastic comment. Model 1 and Model 2 share most of architecture, the difference is the choice LSTM layer of GRU layer. Model 3 to Model 6 are our attempts to tune the hyperparameters. Model 7 is our attempt to add a second recurrent chain to our model.

We have two sequence embedding layer for 'comment' and 'parent_comment' inputs, one integer input and one categorical embedding layer input, we will first concatenate the sequnce embedding layers and pass to a dense layer to lower down dimension, then we concatenate it with two other inputs, then pass it to dense layers and final output layer.
